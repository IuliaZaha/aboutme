Investigating the sense of presence and its effects on learning with SimX Technology

  1. Home
  2. Investigating the sense of presence and its effects on learning with SimX Technology

Project information

  • Category: Research
  • Tools: Oculus Quest 2, CAMIL framework
  • Project date: November, 2022
  • Project URL: www.xreffect.net

Description

The use of imrnersive virtual reality technology (IVR) has been shown to be a popular method for educational and training purposes lately. This has resulted in an upsurge of studies in this area, however little work that compresses the findings and observations into theoretical frameworks has been done, and even fewer studies to certify these frameworks and investigate their potential. Moreover, medical staff and teachers seem to be skeptic towards the use of IVR in medical training generally.

In this research, participants engaged with two IVR medical emergency scenarios provided by SimX technology, their knowledge being measured before and after the IVR experience along with the sense of presence experienced, according to the Cognitive Affective Model of Immersive Virtual Reality (CAMIL).

Finally, participants gave heir opinion towards SimX IVR technology, stating that it could be a useful additional tool to the current training.

Experiment results:

  • Participants felt a high sense of presence
  • The Cognitive Affective Factors triggered by Presence affect the Learning Rate (33.3%) - could be leveraged if VR practice is regularized
  • SimX Technology can be used an additional learning tool in institutions
  • SimX is a tool for practicing medical emergency protocols, learning to communicate with the patient and recall information when in emergency
